The Mastery Assessment

A seven-dimension self-assessment for measuring your energy mastery capabilities across the full spectrum of leadership presence.

On a scale of 1–6 (6 being high), how well, how much, or how often do you...

This assessment is drawn from our work with leaders across fifty years and over fifty organizations. It measures the seven dimensions of energy mastery that correspond to the body's natural energy centers — from the grounded stability of the sacrum to the inspired openness of the crown.

There are no right answers. The purpose is honest self-inquiry — to diagnose where you are, declare where you want to go, and design a practice that gets you there.

Dimension 1 · Sacrum

Balance — The Power of Aligned Action

Center / Relax / Ground — Simplicity, Substance, Stability

  • Maintain balance between personal and professional life?
  • Hold yourself and others accountable for commitments?
  • Maintain a measurable, personal mastery practice?
  • Center and relax when in pressure situations?
  • Live simply with a feeling of stability?
Dimension 2 · Belly

Flexibility — The Power of Non-Resistance

Allow / Accept / Adapt — Suppleness, Subtlety, Sustainability

  • Accept extended periods of ambiguity?
  • Easily let go of anger, resentment, or sadness?
  • Approach conflict as a creative opportunity?
  • Feel a sense of suppleness in the body?
  • Adapt to sudden, unexpected change?
Dimension 3 · Solar Plexus

Vitality — The Power of Dynamic Presence

Breathe / Sense / Enjoy — Sensitivity, Sensuality, Spontaneity

  • Practice conscious body presence or breath control?
  • Enjoy sensuality? Enjoy spontaneity?
  • Wake up feeling positive in the morning?
  • Practice a varied exercise program?
  • Practice balanced daily nutrition?
  • Enjoy your daily life?
Dimension 4 · Heart

Love — The Power of Relational Harmony

Feel / Flow / Join — Sincerity, Sympathy, Synergy

  • Follow your bliss?
  • Sustain harmony in your family relations?
  • Demonstrate empathy for people who counter you?
  • Provide mentoring and coaching to those around you?
  • Demonstrate courage in difficult, conflict situations?
  • Collaborate creatively in work teams?
  • Listen deeply to others?
Dimension 5 · Throat

Voice — The Power of Creative Expression

Declare / Engage / Dialogue — Silence, Sharing, Song

  • Declare your mission, vision, and goals to others?
  • Engage in appreciative, generative dialogue with others?
  • Engage in the practice of a fine art or craft?
  • Develop your strengths? Sing your song?
  • Listen to your listening?
Dimension 6 · Brow

Vision — The Power of Inspired Leadership

Imagine / Intend / Create — Stewardship, Service, Strategy

  • Visualize a transcendent purpose for your life?
  • Provide principled leadership aspiring to the improbable?
  • Set time aside for imagining and break-set thinking?
  • Generate clear strategy? Make decisions under pressure?
  • Fulfill the goals you set out for yourself?
  • Provide generous stewardship?
Dimension 7 · Crown

Inspiration — The Power of Not Knowing

Inquire / Listen / Intuit — Solitude, Stillness, Serenity

  • Value self-reflection and inquiry into your own dynamics?
  • Value not knowing? Value paradox? Value intuition?
  • Seek or listen to a source of guidance beyond yourself?
  • Practice a form of meditation, contemplation, or prayer?
  • Demonstrate authenticity and integrity in your affairs?
  • Enjoy silence and solitude in nature?

Designing Your Mastery Practice

The assessment is just the beginning. Here's how to turn diagnosis into development.

I

Diagnose

Complete the mastery assessment. Identify your strengths and development edges.

II

Declare

Declare your development vision. Set measurable goals for each dimension.

III

Select

Choose mastery practices that serve your goals. Plan a playful practice routine.

IV

Enroll

Enlist practice partners and a circle of supporters to help you stay on track.

V

Review

Practice weekly. Review progress regularly. Adjust based on results.

Practice only exists in the present. Practice diligently, but for the sake of the practice. Dedicate your effort without attachment to outcomes. Love the plateaus that show no progress — they are a good sign of progress. Play at the edge between competence and the unknown. Gentle perseverance pays.
