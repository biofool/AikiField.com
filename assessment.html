The Security Leadership Assessment

Measure your security maturity and the leadership presence capabilities that sustain it.

Two assessments in one. The first measures your organization's security maturity. The second measures the leadership presence capabilities that determine whether your program holds up under pressure.

On a scale of 1–6 (6 being high), how well, how much, or how often do you...

There are no right answers. The purpose is honest self-inquiry — to diagnose where you are, envision where you want to go, and design a practice that gets you there.

Part 1: Security Maturity Assessment

Five categories measuring your organization's security posture, controls, and readiness.

Governance & Strategy

  • Have a documented security posture aligned to your business stage?
  • Have a security roadmap with clear priorities and owners?
  • Review security metrics with the leadership team regularly?
  • Have a risk register that's actively maintained and visible?

Product & Engineering Security

  • Conduct threat modeling for new products and features?
  • Have documented secure development practices your team follows?
  • Have an AI governance framework for AI-powered features?
  • Integrate security into your CI/CD pipeline?

DevSecOps & Vulnerability Remediation

  • Track average time to identify, escalate, and remediate vulnerabilities?
  • Use AI-assisted triage workflows for vulnerability management?
  • Have a defined vulnerability severity ranking and SLA?
  • Monitor your DevOps pipeline for security issues?

Customer Trust & Compliance

  • Can complete enterprise security questionnaires without panic?
  • Have a clear narrative for how you protect customer data?
  • Are SOC 2 (or equivalent) ready or certified?
  • Turn security from a deal-blocker into a differentiator?

Incident Readiness

  • Have an incident response playbook with defined roles?
  • Conduct tabletop exercises to test your incident response?
  • Have a customer communication plan for security incidents?
  • Feel confident in your team's ability to lead through a breach?

Part 2: Leadership Presence Assessment

Seven dimensions measuring the leadership presence capabilities that sustain your security program under pressure.

Dimension 1 · Balance

Aligned Action — Center / Relax / Ground

  • Maintain balance between security urgency and business priorities?
  • Hold yourself and your team accountable for security commitments?
  • Center and relax when in high-pressure security situations?
  • Keep security decisions simple and grounded in business reality?
Dimension 2 · Flexibility

Non-Resistance — Allow / Accept / Adapt

  • Accept extended periods of uncertainty about emerging threats?
  • Let go of security approaches that no longer serve?
  • Approach security conflicts with engineering as creative opportunities?
  • Adapt to sudden security challenges without freezing?
Dimension 3 · Vitality

Dynamic Presence — Breathe / Sense / Enjoy

  • Practice conscious presence before high-stakes security conversations?
  • Bring energy and engagement to security discussions?
  • Maintain the stamina for sustained security leadership?
  • Find genuine enjoyment in the craft of security leadership?
Dimension 4 · Heart

Relational Harmony — Feel / Flow / Join

  • Build genuine partnership with engineering?
  • Demonstrate empathy for engineers who raise security concerns?
  • Collaborate creatively with product on secure feature design?
  • Listen deeply to what customers are actually asking about security?
Dimension 5 · Voice

Creative Expression — Aspire / Engage / Dialogue

  • Articulate your security posture and commitments clearly to others?
  • Communicate security risk in language the business can understand?
  • Advocate for security investment without using fear as a weapon?
  • Listen to your own communication patterns and adjust?
Dimension 6 · Vision

Inspired Leadership — Imagine / Intend / Create

  • Visualize a security program that enables business growth?
  • Set time aside for strategic security thinking?
  • Make sound security decisions under pressure?
  • Provide generous stewardship of customer trust?
Dimension 7 · Inspiration

Not Knowing — Inquire / Listen / Intuit

  • Value self-reflection and inquiry into your own security leadership?
  • Value not knowing? Value paradox? Value intuition about emerging risk?
  • Listen to signals from the threat landscape beyond the obvious?
  • Find quiet space to sense emerging risks before they materialize?

Designing Your Security Leadership Practice

The assessment is just the beginning. Here's how to turn diagnosis into development.

I — Diagnose

Complete both assessments. Identify your security maturity gaps and leadership edges.

II — Aspire

Set your security posture target and leadership development vision. Measurable goals.

III — Select

Choose the security controls and leadership practices that serve your goals. Plan a practical routine.

IV — Enroll

Enlist your leadership team and supporters to stay on track and measure effectiveness.

V — Review

Practice and review regularly. Adjust based on results, incidents, and changing conditions.
