Engagement Process

A clear path from where you are to where you want to be.

The structures and phases that guide every engagement — each one there to give you a concrete result, not more process.

Leadership agreement

Agreement II · Execution

Management agreement

Six phases to a program that fits

A collaborative design cycle tailored to your stage, risks, and customers.

I

Connecting our circle

Get aligned on what security means for your business.

Establish trust, shared language, and a clear picture of the current state — your real risks and what customers actually ask about.

II

Sensing the field

Map where you're exposed, strong, and gapped.

Your attack surface, threat model, compliance obligations, customer expectations, and AI risk exposure.

III

Finding the priorities

Surface the 20% of work that removes 80% of your risk.

Let the picture settle and create space for the right priorities to emerge — the highest-leverage moves first.

IV

Designing the program

Build the minimum viable program that covers real risk.

Policies, controls, and practices without enterprise overhead — what's first, what's next, what can wait.

V

Setting the posture

Turn security into a customer-trust differentiator.

Agree the posture vision internally and externally, clarify goals and owners, and prepare the customer-facing narrative.

VI

Cycling

Keep the program alive as you grow and threats shift.

Security is a living practice — return to earlier phases as your business, the threat landscape, and AI capabilities evolve.
