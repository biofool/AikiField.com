Engagement Process

The structures, agreements, and phases that guide every transformation — from individual coaching to whole-system change.

The Alignment Covenant

Four critical agreements that create the container for transformation. Based on living-systems leadership principles, these require articulation, commitment, and regular review by the senior team.

Agreement I — Declaration

The Leadership Agreement

Establishes identity, direction, and strategic awareness.

  • Contextual awareness & higher purpose
  • Compelling vision & mission declaration
  • Clear cultural identity
  • Living-systems view & complex pattern recognition
  • Strategic thinking & mission-critical navigation
Agreement II — Execution

The Management Agreement

Establishes the operating discipline for extraordinary results.

  • Integrated operating plan & open data system
  • Disciplined measurement & clear accountabilities
  • Embodied commitment & regular review
  • Extraordinary results in real time
Agreement III — Change

The Development Agreement

Establishes the conditions for growth and resilience.

  • Deep trust, high connectivity, mutual mentorship
  • Collaborative teams & mutual risk
  • Continual employee engagement
  • Stakeholder relationship building & influence planning
  • Breakdowns as opportunities for breakthrough
Agreement IV — Inspiration

The Inquiry Agreement

Establishes the conditions for innovation and emergence.

  • Leadership presence & personal mastery
  • Valuing paradox, not-knowing & emergence
  • Intuitive declaration & courageous conversation
  • Deep listening & collective intelligence
  • Sensing fields & sourcing the creative spirit

The Transformation Design Cycle

Six phases for facilitating the intuitive design and practical implementation of breakthrough systemic results. Transformation from the inside out.

Phase I — Connecting Our Circle

Reflective dialogue. Who are we? Establishing safety, trust, and heart connection. Energy breathing, centering, grounding. The Circle of Requests — surfacing strengths and coaching opportunities.

Phase II — Sensing the Field of Play

For whom do we create value? Visualize the system constellation. Sense forces, flows, and patterns. Raise critical questions. Share data. Intuitive visioning — sensing openings for possibility.

Phase III — Incubating for Emergence

Letting go. Wandering in nature. Time out of mind. Sleeping on it. Creating the spaciousness that allows intuitive insight to surface naturally.

Phase IV — Designing the Transformation

Select challenge arenas. Imagine possible breakthroughs. Prioritize. Select a potential breakthrough and strategize critical pilot initiatives.

Phase V — Declaring the Transformation

Agree on and declare a breakthrough vision and strategic initiatives. Clarify goals, priority objectives, and accountabilities. Intuitive declaration — commit to the impossible as probable.

Phase VI — Cycling

Cycle, cycle, cycle. The design cycle is not linear — it's iterative. Return to earlier phases as needed. Fill in holes, check progress, refresh commitments, and coach to needs.

Core Tools

Three tools woven through every phase of the design cycle:

Deep Somatic Presence

Centering, grounding, flowing. The facilitator's own presence is key to the realization of all phases.

Somatic Team Dialogue

Four levels of attention: content, process, inner, systemic. Dialogue for inquiry; discussion for closure.

Intuitive Declaration

Committing to the impossible as probable. "I declare that whatever I say is possible is possible."

The Crucible

Multi-force simulations that test leadership declarations under pressure. Used before training to reveal reactive patterns, and after to test integration of new capabilities.

Four Levels of Attention in Dialogue

The facilitator's capacity to hold attention at multiple levels simultaneously is what deepens dialogue from surface exchange to collective intelligence.

I

Content Attention

What's seen and said. Clarity of goal and dialogue type. Collecting divergent views. Maintaining the spirit of inquiry.

II

Process Attention

How we're playing. Facilitation protocols and tools. Maintaining consciousness of location on the spectrum. Summarizing for navigation clarity.

III

Inner Attention

Intuiting. Sensing energy fields. Maintaining embodied presence. Listening to your listening. Mental models and reactivity triggers.

IV

Systemic Attention

View of the whole. What is unseen and unsaid. Listening for the voice of the whole. Surfacing collective intelligence and emergent consensus.

Three Levels of Commitment

How teams contract for results — from the known to the impossible.

Promise

Guarantee. You know how and when to achieve the objective based on past experience. Linear extrapolation from known capability.

Stretch

Aspiration. You're not sure how to achieve the objective. Minimal past experience. Extended linear extrapolation beyond comfort.

Breakthrough

Declaration. You don't know how to achieve it. It's never been done before. Non-linear extrapolation — declaring the impossible probable.

Finite & Infinite Games

A distinction that shapes how organizations play. Are you playing to win, or playing to keep the game going?

Finite Players

  • Play for the purpose of winning
  • Rules may not change
  • Play within the boundaries
  • Are serious
  • Win titles
  • Play to be powerful
  • Are theatrical
  • Consume time

Infinite Players

  • Play for the purpose of continuing the play
  • Rules must change
  • Play with the boundaries
  • Are playful
  • Have nothing but their names
  • Play with strength
  • Are dramatic
  • Create time
