## Page Header
**Title:** Our Approach
**Subtitle:** A somatic, experiential methodology for developing leaders who can source power, harmonize teams, and navigate the unknown.

## One Cardinal Assumption

**Energy follows attention.**

Mastery of attention is mastery of energy. A leader's presence — the quality of their attention, the state of their being — is the single most powerful influence on the climate of their organization. Before strategy, before communication, before action: presence.

## Shifting Domains of Awareness

**Subtitle:** The core of our work is a shift in the domain from which a leader operates.

### From: Conditioned Mind
The operating system of thought — the source of reactivity patterns.
- Automatic reactions to pressure
- Defensive patterns and shadow behaviors
- Habitual narrowing of perception under stress
- Identity locked in a fixed self-concept
- Leading from fear, control, or force

### To: Unconditioned Presence
Embodied awareness — the source of intuitive, compassionate action.
- Grounded, centered response to pressure
- Open, inclusive perception of the whole field
- Spacious awareness that welcomes the unknown
- Identity as open consciousness, not fixed persona
- Leading from presence, harmony, and inquiry

## Three Principles

**Subtitle:** The qualities of leadership we cultivate.

### I — Inspired Presence
The power of grounded, vital, embodied leadership. When a leader is deeply present — centered, grounded, flowing — they become a stabilizing force in any storm. This is command presence that comes from *being*, not from rank or role.

### II — Relational Harmony
The power of compassionate collaboration. Beyond empathy: a felt-sense of the relational field, the ability to blend with opposing forces, join with diverse perspectives, and co-create solutions that serve the whole system.

### III — Open Inquiry
The power of not-knowing. The willingness to release certainty, suspend assumptions, and let insight emerge. This is the source of breakthrough innovation — declaring the impossible probable, and leading from intuition rather than extrapolation.

## Three Practices

**Subtitle:** How the principles are developed — through direct, embodied experience.

### Practice I — Opening the Body
**Energize — Center / Ground / Flow**

Somatic exercises that develop grounded presence: body scanning, centering, grounding, energy flow, and the capacity to remain centered under physical pressure. The body becomes the primary instrument for sensing and sourcing power.
- Centering — plumb line, wobble, alignment
- Grounding — sinking open, roots & branches
- Flowing — hand sensing, extending, expanding field
- Tests — partner push, unbendable arm, walking backward

### Practice II — Opening the Heart
**Harmonize — Blend / Join / Enhance**

Partner practices that develop relational resonance: sensing the field between people, stepping off the line of conflict, blending with opposing energy, and leading co-creation through deep listening.
- Sensing relational energies — the approach
- Opening listening space — off the line, settle & engage
- Leading listening — blending energies
- Leading co-creation — double grab, co-discovery

### Practice III — Opening the Mind
**Navigate — Inquire / Not Knowing / Intuit**

Inquiry practices that develop intuitive navigation: suspending certainty, valuing paradox, sensing for emergence, and declaring breakthroughs without evidence. The crucible — multi-force simulations — tests all three practices under pressure.
- The crucible — multi-force leadership simulations
- Intuitive declaration — committing to the impossible
- Field sensing — intuitive strategic play
- Mastery practice design — personal commitments

## The Crucible — Learning Under Pressure

**The Crucible:** A slow-motion simulation of leadership under the pressure of complex, unpredictable change. Multiple forces converge on a single responder who must manage each force calmly, lead the group compassionately, and discover how to innovate intuitively in the midst of the melee. Used before training, the crucible reveals each person's go-to reactive style. Used after training, it tests whether new skills have been embodied and integrated.

Our methodology is fundamentally experiential. Participants don't just *hear* about presence — they *feel* it. They don't just *discuss* collaboration — they *practice* it with partners. And they don't just *think* about navigating change — they *experience* it in the crucible.

This somatic, self-validating approach bypasses conditioned views and helps people see the world anew. The ability to generate breakthrough performance comes not from new information, but from a new way of being.

## The Collaboration Framework

**Subtitle:** The discourse, decision-making, and dialogue tools that enable teams to navigate complexity together.

### The Discourse Spectrum
Effective teams know when to dialogue (to deepen understanding and explore the unknown) and when to discuss (to argue positions and arrive at closure). The key is maintaining consciousness of where you are on the spectrum.

**Spectrum:** Dialogue → Open · Generate · Reflect · Strategize · Negotiate · Debate · Decide → Discussion

### Dialogue vs. Discussion

| Dialogue | Discussion |
|---|---|
| Deepen understanding | Argue positions |
| Explore the unknown | Promote the known |
| Raise questions | Answer questions |
| Examine assumptions | Defend assumptions |
| Listening | Speaking |
| Flow of shared meaning | Shadow of unmet needs |
| Inquire / Sense / Learn | Advocate / Sell / Persuade |
| Unfold complexity | Arrive at closure |
| Consider | Decide |

## Four Levels of Attention in Dialogue

**Subtitle:** The facilitator's capacity to hold attention at multiple levels simultaneously is what deepens dialogue from surface exchange to collective intelligence.

### I — Content Attention
What's seen and said. Clarity of goal and dialogue type. Collecting divergent views. Maintaining the spirit of inquiry.

### II — Process Attention
How we're playing. Facilitation protocols and tools. Maintaining consciousness of location on the spectrum. Summarizing for navigation clarity.

### III — Inner Attention
Intuiting. Sensing energy fields. Maintaining embodied presence. Listening to your listening. Mental models and reactivity triggers.

### IV — Systemic Attention
View of the whole. What is unseen and unsaid. Listening for the voice of the whole. Surfacing collective intelligence and emergent consensus.

## Three Levels of Commitment

**Subtitle:** How teams contract for results — from the known to the impossible.

### Promise
**Guarantee.** You know how and when to achieve the objective based on past experience. Linear extrapolation from known capability.

### Stretch
**Aspiration.** You're not sure how to achieve the objective. Minimal past experience. Extended linear extrapolation beyond comfort.

### Breakthrough
**Declaration.** You don't know how to achieve it. It's never been done before. Non-linear extrapolation — declaring the impossible probable.

### Infinite Players

- Rules must change

On a scale of 1–6 (6 being high), how well, how much, or how often do you...

This assessment is drawn from our work with leaders across fifty years and over fifty organizations. It measures the seven dimensions of energy mastery that correspond to the body's natural energy centers — from the grounded stability of the sacrum to the inspired openness of the crown.

There are no right answers. The purpose is honest self-inquiry — to diagnose where you are, declare where you want to go, and design a practice that gets you there.

- Live simply with a feeling of stability?

## Designing Your Mastery Practice

**Subtitle:** The assessment is just the beginning. Here's how to turn diagnosis into development.

### I — Diagnose
Complete the mastery assessment. Identify your strengths and development edges.

### II — Declare
Declare your development vision. Set measurable goals for each dimension.

### III — Select
Choose mastery practices that serve your goals. Plan a playful practice routine.

### IV — Enroll
Enlist practice partners and a circle of supporters to help you stay on track.

### V — Review
Practice weekly. Review progress regularly. Adjust based on results.

Love the plateaus that show no progress — they are a good sign of progress. Play at the edge between competence and the unknown. Gentle perseverance pays.
